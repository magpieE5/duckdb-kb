# UO IS Layoffs & Organizational Impact

Multiple rounds of layoffs at UO Information Services, creating flattened management structure and risk-averse culture.

**Management flattening:**
- Brock's boss now manages 18 people
- Several other teams similarly flat
- Result: Limited bandwidth to explain technical details

**Layoff targeting:**
- People who've been around a long time
- Non-unionized positions (easier to lay off)
- Some unionized in unique positions where layoff won't trigger cascading bumping effect

**Not as severe as other schools** in their consortium/region, but still significant impact.

**Cultural impact:**
- Risk aversion increases post-layoffs
- "Nobody gets fired for buying Microsoft" mentality strengthens
- Vendor-first stance reinforced despite technical arguments for alternatives
- Bandwidth constraints limit ability to explore novel approaches

**Survival architecture characteristics:**
- Technical debt accumulation (can't afford to clean up)
- Vendor lock-in deepens (safer to stick with known)
- Innovation constrained by limited capacity
- Knowledge concentration (when experts leave, gaps appear)

**Relevance to KB project:** This is the environment institutional knowledge capture must operate in - limited time, risk aversion, knowledge turnover accelerating.
