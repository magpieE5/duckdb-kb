# Sports Information Research - NCAA/NFL Rankings

**Source:** Web search execution
**Date:** 2025-11-20
**Context:** First sports research for student user - documenting methodology and learnings

---

## Research Approach

**Query Strategy:**
Executed 4 parallel searches to gather comprehensive football information:
1. NCAA college football rankings (current Top 25)
2. NFL standings and playoff picture
3. NFL player statistical leaders
4. Heisman Trophy candidates

**Parallel execution advantage:** Gathered all major angles simultaneously rather than sequential searches.

---

## Key Learnings About Sports Information Structure

**College Football Hierarchy:**
- Multiple ranking systems exist (CFP Committee, AP Poll, Coaches Poll)
- CFP Committee rankings matter most for playoff selection
- Rankings released weekly during season (Tuesdays)
- Playoff expansion context important for 2025 season

**NFL Information Organization:**
- Playoff picture based on conference standings (AFC/NFC separate)
- Tiebreakers matter significantly (conference record, head-to-head)
- Season structure: 18 weeks regular season, playoffs follow
- Player rankings: both peer-voted (Top 100) and statistical leaders

**Heisman Trophy Context:**
- Primarily QB award in modern era (but historically open to all positions)
- Performance-based + team success matters
- Defensive players rarely win (last: Charles Woodson, 1997)
- Odds shift weekly based on performances

---

## Adapting for Student Context

**Considerations for 7th grader:**
- Football knowledge level unknown - provided context without oversimplifying
- Organized by clear hierarchy (Top 10, not full Top 25 details)
- Highlighted standout players with memorable achievements (record-breaking stats)
- Balanced college and pro information (interests could span both)

**Future refinements:**
- Ask about specific teams/players of interest to narrow focus
- Learn whether college or pro football is primary interest
- Understand if playing football influences information needs (positions, techniques, strategy)

---

## Search Quality Assessment

**Successful elements:**
- Current information (Week 13 college, Week 11 NFL = November 2025)
- Major sources captured (ESPN, CBS Sports, NCAA.com, NFL.com)
- Both team and individual player information gathered

**Information gaps:**
- Complete NFL standings with win-loss records not in snippets
- Detailed individual player statistics required clicking through to sources
- Would need follow-up searches for specific team/player deep dives

---

## Pattern Recognition: Sports Research vs Technical Research

**Differences noted:**
- **Temporal specificity critical** - "2025 season Week 13" vs generic technical docs
- **Multiple authoritative sources** - No single source of truth like official documentation
- **Rapid information decay** - Rankings change weekly, requires fresh searches
- **Narrative context matters** - Upsets, comebacks, injuries affect interpretation

**Technical research parallels:**
- Breaking complex topic into targeted sub-queries (NCAA/NFL/players/awards)
- Cross-referencing multiple sources for completeness
- Understanding hierarchical information structure
