# S2 Opening Questions - PDS Adoption Story

Prepared questions for S2 session to explore PDS organizational resistance story. Brock explicitly queued this topic for next session.

## Questions

### Timeline & Trigger
- When did PDS shift from "interesting experiment" to "controversial"? 
- Was there a specific incident, or gradual institutional antibodies forming?

### Stakeholder Map
- Who specifically is blocking broader adoption? 
  - Travis (your boss)?
  - Vacant Associate CIO position creating leadership vacuum?
  - DBAs protecting Oracle turf?
  - Other team members invested in current stack?
- Who are your allies, if any?

### Navigation Strategy
- How are you playing this? 
  - Quiet persistence?
  - Active advocacy?
  - Strategic patience waiting for leadership change?
  - Building external credibility until internal resistance becomes untenable?

### Constraint Impact
- What does "personal use only" actually prevent? 
- What can't you build/deploy/share that you want to?
- Is Sameer (other Data Engineer) truly free to adopt it, or is "allowed" doing heavy lifting there?

### Theory of Change
- What's your read on how this resolves? 
  - Vacant Associate CIO hire shifts politics?
  - External adoption creates FOMO?
  - Vendor migration forces the issue?
  - Or are you planning your own exit?

### Satisfaction Cost
- How much does this constrain/frustrate you versus energize you (building the future anyway)?

### External Validation Paradox
- When other schools adopt your work while yours won't - what does that tell you about the organization, your positioning, or higher ed generally?

## Session Plan

Open S2 with these questions unless Brock redirects to different topic.

Expected themes:
- Institutional resistance patterns in risk-averse orgs
- Technical leadership in politically constrained environments
- External validation as change lever
- Homegrown vs. vendor solutions
- Strategic patience vs. active advocacy navigation
