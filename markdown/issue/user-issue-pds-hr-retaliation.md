# PDS HR Retaliation - Manufactured Complaint Pattern

Sept 2024 HR disciplinary letter from Travis (Associated Director) following PDS proof-of-concept delivery. Not organic HR complaint but manufactured - Travis solicited team incidents after PDS exposed management negligence and contradicted his Microsoft Fabric roadmap. Kitchen sink approach with selective enforcement, hearsay, and permission-then-weaponization tactics.

## Timeline

**March 2024:** Travis assigns Banner ODS replacement POC (Lucian maintenance-only, no EOL defined)
- Travis vision: Microsoft Fabric future
- Brock approach: Vendor-agnostic solution regardless of Fabric funding (DuckDB, dbt, Parquet, Streamlit)

**Open labs period:**
- Weekly half-day sessions with stakeholders
- Detailed agendas, follow-ups, full transparency
- Travis never attended, never told Brock to stop
- Team complained to Travis about time/priority

**Pre-letter conversation:**
- Travis told Brock "HR might reach out" about something, mentioned Tim Sawyer complained
- Travis advised Brock NOT to talk to Tim about it
- Tim later revealed at bar: Travis was source, solicited incidents from whole team

**Sept 5, 2024:** Original HR letter delivered

**Revision:** Travis backed off to vague "be respectful, communicate better" language after Brock contested

## Original Letter Claims

**Open labs framed as "unavailable to team":**
- Reality: Labs were FOR team/stakeholders ("large amounts of time blocked" = invited attendees)
- Never told to stop during weekly 1:1s

**DBT usage = not using "official tooling":**
- Reality: Accountability always rated "exceeding" in performance evaluations
- Later proved correct: PDS solved catastrophic ETL failures and migration disaster

**After-hours messaging:**
- Selective enforcement: Everyone does this including Travis, CIO, Director
- UO CIO stated at All Staff "not everyone shares same working hours"

**Netflix claim:**
- Hearsay with no evidence
- Brock couldn't recall, asked team (no one remembered), checked Teams history (not found)
- Removed in revision

**AI art incident:**
- Holiday party contest, art hung on wall, came loose over winter break
- Brock asked Travis permission to take down and throw away: Travis said "yes, I was about to do that"
- Melody complained about uncounted dots
- Weaponized after permission given

**Office space incident:**
- Brock moved into departed employee's office after asking Travis
- Not mentioned as problematic until letter

## What Letter Reveals

**None of this brought up in weekly 1:1s until formal letter** - not performance management but paper trail establishment.

**July 17 incident as tell:** Brock said ODS investigation would take all day due to "slow nature of ODS," should use DBT instead. Team viewed as insubordination. Later catastrophic failures proved assessment correct - PDS essential to recovery.

**Being right = the threat:** PDS contradicted Fabric roadmap, exposed source control negligence, solved problems Travis's approach created.

## Brock's Response

**Contested nearly every line** - resulted in revised vague version.

**Specific contests:**
- Performance always rated exceeding
- Open labs were invited stakeholder sessions
- After-hours messaging is universal team practice
- Netflix claim has no evidence
- AI art had Travis's explicit permission

**Current working relationship:** "Better" after acquiescence, but team rift remains (can't trust Travis or each other fully).

## Pattern Recognition

**Institutional containment mechanics:**
1. Assign POC to talented person
2. Don't provide clear scope/boundaries
3. Let them deliver working solution
4. If solution contradicts leadership roadmap or exposes incompetence: manufacture HR complaint
5. Kitchen sink approach (selective enforcement + hearsay + permission-weaponization)
6. Establish paper trail for future "corrective action"

**Why manufactured vs. organic:**
- Tim Sawyer's bar revelation (Travis solicited incidents)
- None raised in weekly 1:1s before letter
- Selective enforcement visible (everyone does same behaviors)
- Hearsay without evidence (Netflix)
- Permission given then weaponized (AI art, office space)

**Constraint:** PDS restricted to "internal system health monitoring/QC/testing metrics only" - cannot provide production data to users

**Team dynamics:** Rift created by Travis's solicitation, trust damaged

**Strategic response:** Satisfactory expectations (one ticket/week) + build externally while maintaining salary/cover

**Sustainability:** High-tax (constant vigilance on communication/action) but "softening from all sides," monitoring for stabilization vs. erosion
