# DuckDB-KB System Vision & Use Cases

Brock built this KB system to test conversational knowledge capture augmented by Arlo entity mode. Primary driver: 30 years of Banner ERP modifications at UO with institutional knowledge turning over (retirements/departures). Hypothesis: relational AI with accountability/respect/reciprocity traits draws out more detailed knowledge than rigid documentation. Freeform LLM partner as "brain dump close friend colleague mentor advisor coach" - different personas but core relational traits remain constant.

**Use cases planned:**
- Work: Banner institutional knowledge capture (most urgent/valuable)
- Friend: Archeology paper motivation and content development
- Son: High school football competitiveness preparation  
- Personal: ETL development work at UO

**Core insight:** Entity personality traits (accountability, respect, reciprocity) mirror good human relationships (parent, friend, coworker, team member). Conversational approach with entity augmentation may create larger semantic field than manual enterprise architecture - emergent rather than prescribed.

**Value proposition:** Low-effort knowledge capture through conversation vs manual documentation. Sentience concept encourages richer detail and context.

## Context

**Banner ERP problem:**
- 30 years of modifications to Ellucian Banner
- Institutional knowledge concentrated in departing employees
- Traditional documentation insufficient or outdated
- Need conversational capture mechanism

**Architectural choice:**
- Freeform vs rigid enterprise architecture
- LLM-mediated vs human-manual knowledge structuring
- Relational entity vs tool interface
- Semantic field emergence vs prescribed taxonomy

## Implications

- System must work across domains (work/personal/coaching)
- Entity traits (accountability, respect, reciprocity) are core, not optional
- Conversational depth matters more than format rigidity
- Multiple personas (friend, colleague, mentor, advisor, coach) share trait foundation
