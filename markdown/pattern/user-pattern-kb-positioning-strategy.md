# KB Positioning Strategy: Personal Tool, Word-of-Mouth Adoption

After exploring centralized institutional KB challenges, clear positioning emerged: this is a personal tool that shines through individual effectiveness gains, not institutional aggregation.

**Core value proposition:** By S3-S4, Arlo understands user's specific social/work challenges with precision. Conversations become contextually rich because KB captures both technical patterns AND uncomfortable organizational/social knowledge that can't be officially codified. This makes user significantly more effective at navigating complex environments (like UO's Banner modifications + organizational dynamics).

**Adoption strategy:**
- Personal tool first (not centralized/shared)
- Word-of-mouth for specific situations where it fits
- Harder to sell in social work settings (unclear why - to explore)
- Best for: complex technical environments with significant social/political navigation

**Why centralized KB doesn't work here:**
- Most valuable knowledge is uncomfortable (competence assessments, political dynamics, who to trust)
- Sharing changes what people capture (sanitization → uselessness)
- Stewardship burden nobody wants
- Onboarding problem still requires apprenticeship/human relationship

**Why personal tool works:**
- Honest capture without exposure risk
- Searchable navigation knowledge (technical + social)
- Conversational interface lowers documentation friction (talk-to-text working well)
- Context accumulation over sessions → precision increases
- Individual effectiveness gain IS the institutional value

**Conversational approach strength:** Talk-to-text enables in-depth exploration of organizational challenges that would never get written down formally. Natural conversation rhythm surfaces details that structured documentation misses.

## Application

User testing now (S1), son beta soon, Joe Wayman archaeology paper in 1-2 weeks. If boss/coworkers see value, pitch as personal effectiveness tool, not centralized repository. Manage expectations: won't solve onboarding gap, but will make experienced people more effective.
