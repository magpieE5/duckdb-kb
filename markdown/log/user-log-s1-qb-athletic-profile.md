# S1: Rowan's QB Athletic Profile Assessment

Comprehensive athletic assessment from Session 1, establishing baseline abilities and development pathway. Rowan Prouty, 7th grade QB, Spencer Butte Middle School.

## Athletic Profile Summary

**Position:** Quarterback (primary), Wide Receiver (backup if needed)
**Grade:** 7th (age ~12-13)
**Development Style:** Pocket Passer (arm talent pathway, not dual-threat)

## Physical Assessment

### Arm Strength: ELITE
**Stationary throw:** 50 yards
- Benchmark for ages 13-15: 35-40 yards
- Rowan is 10+ yards ABOVE benchmark
- Elite for middle school, approaching high school levels

**Throwing on run:** 30 yards
- Excellent mobility and off-platform throwing
- Shows body control + core strength
- Critical skill for extending plays

**Analysis:** 
- Elite natural arm talent
- Already has college-level arm strength for age
- Rare to show this much arm strength this early

### Accuracy: ELITE
**Consistency:** Hits WR in chest "almost every throw"
- This is elite accuracy for any age
- Most young QBs have arm strength OR accuracy, rarely both
- Shows natural ball placement ability

**What this means:**
- Throws are catchable (chest = easiest catch zone)
- Receiver doesn't have to adjust
- Can run after catch immediately
- Minimizes drops

**Next level development:**
- Lead passes (throwing where WR will be)
- Back shoulder throws (away from defender)
- High point (for tall WRs over shorter DBs)
- Directional accuracy (away from coverage)

**Analysis:**
- Combination of 50-yard arm + chest-level accuracy is genuinely rare
- Shows both power and control
- This is the foundation of elite QB potential

### Speed: BELOW AVERAGE
**100 meter sprint:** 15.9 seconds
- Benchmark for age: 12.7-13.9 seconds
- Rowan is ~2-3 seconds slower than average
- Estimated 40-yard dash: 6.0-6.5 seconds (middle school average: 5.3-5.4s)

**Initial claim correction:**
- First said 11.2 seconds (would be elite)
- Corrected to 15.9 seconds
- Important to verify athletic claims with youth

**Impact on QB development:**
- Rules out dual-threat/mobile QB style (Mahomes, Jackson)
- Channels toward pocket passer development (Brady, Manning)
- NOT a dealbreaker - pocket passers can succeed at highest levels
- Speed can improve 1-2 seconds with training + puberty

**Mitigations needed:**
- Elite pocket awareness (feel pressure without seeing)
- Quick release (get ball out fast)
- Excellent decision-making (can't make up mistakes with legs)
- Superior football IQ (know where to throw pre-snap)

### Cross-Training
**Track participation:** Active during track season
- Good for speed development work
- Sprint mechanics training
- Multi-sport athlete benefits
- Structured speed training built in

## Development Pathway Assessment

### QB Style: Pocket Passer
**Reasoning:**
- Elite arm strength + accuracy = foundation
- Below-average speed = can't rely on mobility
- Tom Brady archetype: slow but dominant through IQ + arm talent

**Requirements for success:**
1. Perfect mechanics (already showing good foundation)
2. Elite football IQ (can be developed)
3. Pocket presence (compensate for lack of speed)
4. Quick decision-making (pre-snap reads)
5. Leadership (QB is team leader)

### Strengths to Build On
1. **Elite arm talent** (50 yards) - hardest skill to teach
2. **Elite accuracy** (chest consistency) - shows natural control
3. **Off-platform ability** (30 yards on run) - can extend plays
4. **Analytical mindset** (sports medicine interest suggests smart kid)

### Challenges to Address
1. **Speed limitation** (15.9s 100m) - train but don't expect elite speed
2. **Need QB-specific coaching** - school coaches can't provide position-specific training
3. **Football IQ development** - film study, defensive reads, play recognition
4. **Pocket awareness** - feeling pressure, subtle movement, escape lanes

### Recommended Next Steps

**Immediate (7th-8th grade):**
1. Find QB coach/trainer for position-specific work
2. Start systematic film study (watch pocket passer QBs)
3. Practice footwork daily (ladder drills, cone drills at home)
4. Perfect throwing mechanics before adding power
5. Play organized football (need game reps)
6. Continue track (speed training built in)

**Short-term (entering HS):**
1. Consider QB camps (Elite 11, QB Takeover) if budget allows
2. Build highlight film
3. Maintain strong academics (NCAA eligibility requirements)
4. Develop leadership skills

**Long-term (HS → College):**
1. Research college programs that develop pocket passers
2. Identify QB mentors/role models to study
3. Build recruitment connections early
4. Continue elite arm talent development
